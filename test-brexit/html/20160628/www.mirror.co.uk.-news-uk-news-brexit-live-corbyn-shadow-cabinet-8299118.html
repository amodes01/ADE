Live: Jeremy Corbyn loses no confidence vote as Tories line up to replace David Cameron

Labour MPs held a secret ballot on ousting Jeremy Corbyn while EU leaders gather in Brussels. Follow live updates here

  • All 58 Labour resignations so far08:45

By
Mikey SmithDeputy Political Editor
Dan BloomOnline Political Editor
  • Updated08:12, 29 Jun 2016

Jeremy Corbyn has lost an unprecedented no confidence vote by his own MPs.

The defiant Labour leader tonight claimed the poll had "no constitutional legitimacy" and said he would not "betray" the 60% who voted for him despite MPs voting 172-40 against his leadership.

Despite more than 50 resignations, which led to an awkward meeting of the shadow cabinet, the vote was not binding. See what happens next here.

The Mirror says today that Mr Corbyn, while a decent and principled man, must go for the sake of party unity. Read our reasons here.

Meanwhile David Cameron was well out of the way in Brussels, where EU leaders held crisis meetings today about the fallout from Thursday's Brexit vote.

The Eurocrats are NOT happy - and booed Nigel Farage in the European Parliament after he told them they'd never had a proper job.

Meanwhile the Tory leadership battle reaches fever pitch, with Stephen Crabb becoming the first confirmed candidate.

George Osborne has ruled himself out but Boris Johnson and Theresa May are expected to be leading contenders, with Jeremy Hunt confirming he is considering throwing his hat into the ring.

23:31Mikey Smith

Labour deny party is discussing readmitting George Galloway

A spokesperson for Jeremy Corbyn has denied former MP George Galloway’s membership of the party is “being discussed”.

Galloway was ousted from the party in 2003 for bringing the party into disrepute. He went on to lead the Respect party, sitting as an MP for Bethnal Green and Bow for the party, until he was beaten in the 2015 General Election by Labour’s Naz Shah. He stood unsuccessfully to be Mayor of London this year.

A source described the claim as “complete b******s”

SNP will 'seek to replace Labour' as official opposition

SNP MP Pete Wishart claims his party will seek to replace Labour as the official opposition for PMQs tomorrow.

There have been claims the party would make a move to become the official second party in the wake of Jeremy Corbyn losing the support of all but 39 of his MPs.

Corbyn to appear at Momentum Rally tomorrow

Jeremy Corbyn will appear at a rally organised by pressure group Momentum tomorrow in London.

He’ll be joined by Shadow Chancellor John McDonnell and Shadow Health Secretary Diane Abbott at the rally, to take place at the Trades Union Council’s Congress House, in central London tomorrow from 7pm.

It’s thought he may use the event to kick off his campaign to stay on as leader.

Here’s the full line up of speakers.

  • Jeremy Corbyn MP
  • John McDonnell MP
  • Diane Abbott MP
  • Mark Serwotka, PCS general secretary
  • Rohi Malik, the young Labour activist who introduced Jeremy at party conference
  • Rhea Wolfson, NEC candidate
  • Matt Wrack, FBU general secretary
21:55Mikey Smith

More on the protest outside Parliament

A huge demonstration calling for Britain to remain in the European Union descended on the Houses of Parliament today, despite the rally officially being “cancelled.”

Demonstrators chanted “Down with Boris” and “F*** Farage” in protest at two of the leading figures in the Brexit movement, senior Tory Boris Johnson and Ukip leader Nigel Farage.

Thousands of people were expected to attend events in cities across the UK as dissatisfaction with the decision to leave the bloc manifested itself in plans for public demonstrations.

But organisers have been forced to abandon several gatherings over fears about crowd sizes.

Here’s more on this story

Andy Burnham denies he's quitting Shadow Cabinet

Andy Burnham has denied reports he’s set to resign from Jeremy Corbyn’s Shadow Cabinet.

Channel 4’s Michael Crick tweeted that he had “good sources” saying the Shadow Home Secretary was preparing to jump ship, along with three people appointed to the Shadow Cabinet yesterday.

But Mr Burnham has popped his head up to pour cold water on the rumour.

19:51Mikey Smith

Timetable for Tory leadership election

The Conservative Party have released details of the forthcoming leadership election - which has been pushed back by a week from the presumed timetable.

Here’s the details in full.

“The Party Board welcomes the recommendation of the Executive of the 1922 Committee with regards to the Parliamentary timetable for the selection of the new leader of the Conservative Party. The Board and the 1922 Committee both agree that the leadership election should take place as speedily as practical considerations allow. In order to ensure there is full participation by the membership, the Party Board recommends that the date of the declaration of the leader is the 9th September 2016.”

Timetable:

Wednesday 29 June - Nominations open

Thursday 30 June - Nominations close at noon

Friday 9 September – New leader announced

Ms Eagle has deputised for Jeremy Corbyn at PMQs on occasions when David Cameron has been elsewhere.

Liz McInnes - who voted for Corbyn in today's vote - quits his front bench

Liz McInnes, who was the only person to speak up for Jeremy Corbyn at last night’s meeting of the Parliamentary Labour Party has resigned as a Shadow Minister for Communities and Local Government.

She also voted for Mr Corbyn in this afternoon’s vote of no confidence, but changed her mind after seeing the huge difference between for and against votes, saying 172 to 40 is “too wide a gap” and accepting he’s lost the confidence of MPs.

He’s also stepped back from the review of the Bank of England he was conducting for the party.

Corbyn 'planning rule change' to get rid of rebels

Jeremy Corbyn is reportedly planning some rule changes if he wins the leadership contest.

He’s thought to be planning to either make it easier to remove rebellious MPs or even force “mandatory re-selection.”

Of course, the Labour Party rules already allow him to suspend or expel party members for bringing the party into disrepute.

18:31Mikey Smith

Kevin Brennan has resigned

Kevin Brennan has resigned as Shadow Minister for Trade, Investment, and Intellectual Property.

He’s been an MP since 2001, and has previously served as Further Education minister under Gordon Brown.

18:13Mikey Smith

Scottish Labour leader turns on Jeremy Corbyn

Scottish Labour leader Kezia Dugdale has told BBC Scotland it’s difficult to see how Jeremy Corbyn can remain in place as leader.

She said: “If I lost the confidence of 80% of my parliamentary colleagues, I could not do my job.”

18:05Ben Glaze

Momentum leader: Result of vote is "very regrettable"

James Schneider, of the pro-Corbyn Momentum group, branded the result “really regrettable” and attacked “the plotters”.

“We are at a time of national crisis and what was needed was for the Labour Party to come together to be united and offer a strong voice in these Brexit negotiations,” he said.

“I think the plotters at no point respected the mandate that Jeremy had been given.”

He warned: “Voters will look upon us very, very badly for quite possibly spending however long we are now going to spend at this time of national crisis talking about ourselves.

“It is the old politics of the worst kind.”

Mr Corbyn had been the target of “a very Labour coup”, Mr Schneider told the BBC.

The crisis had unleashed an “incredible destabilisation of the entire Labour Party, a real tarnishing of its image in the country”, he added.

And members who voted for Mr Corbyn “are livid”, he claimed.

“Imploring” Mr Corbyn to quit, former Shadow Justice Minister Andy Slaughter said: “I have huge respect for Jeremy and I agree with him on so much.

“I don’t think he has the experience or the skills to be the leader of a main political party.”

17:45

David Blunkett: Labour will be a suicidal, kamikaze party

Labour grandee David Blunkett has just said Jeremy Corbyn’s team want to turn Labour into a “suicidal, kamikaze party.”

He said: “I haven’t met anyone who is not a supporter of the very far left who doesn’t recognise that we will be annihilated in a general election. It’s a fact.”

He suggested that if Momentum, who he described as a “fringe group” want to have Jeremy Corbyn as leader, they should start their own party, nominate them as leader and see how many votes they get.

Asked if he was saying he thought the party should split, he said: “No, I’m not saying that. I’m saying if they want to have the kind of suicidal, kamikaze party that they are currently indicating that that’s what they want, by remaining in office, then they should set up with Momentum, because that’s their group, the ‘texter’ crowd, they should get that crowd into a party.

“I’m telling them that they should go and that we that endeavour to represent the public - remember the 172, that’s over three quarters of the Parliamentary Labour Party - they represent, of course, the Labour movement, but they represent people out there in their constituencies.”

17:35

Clive Betts: "People tell me they can't vote Labour with Jeremy"

Sheffield MP Clive Betts has told BBC Radio Sheffield his constituents have lost confidence in Jeremy Corbyn’s leadership.

Sheffield voted narrowly for Leave in the EU referendum, and today has already seen Sheffield Central MP Paul Blomfield resign as PPS to Shadow DCLG.

Mr Betts told the station: “People tell me they can’t vote Labour with Jeremy as leader”

Speaking at last night’s meeting of the Parliamentary Labour Party, Margaret Hodge, who presented the motion of no confidence in Jeremy Corbyn, said internal Labour polling had revealed 3 out of 10 people who had voted for the party in May 2015, would not do so now.

17:29

Paul Blomfield and Mary Glindon have resigned

Paul Blomfield (55) and Mary Glinden (56) have stepped down from Jeremy Corbyn’s frontbench.

Both were Parliamentary Private Secretaries, Blomfield for Communities and Local Government and Glindon for Transport.

Mr Blomfield was previously Parliamentary Private Secretary to Hilary Benn, when he was Shadow Leader of the House.

17:23

Momentum organiser warns members are "livid"

James Schneider, of the pro-Corbyn Momentum group, told the BBC the leader was the target of “a very Labour coup”.
The crisis had unleashed an “incredible destabilisation of the entire Labour Party, a real tarnishing of its image in the country”, he added.
And members who voted for Mr Corbyn “are livid”, he warned.

17:23

MP Neil Coyle: 'Overwhelming' vote means Corbyn must goo

MP Neil Coyle told the Mirror Mr Corbyn was “going nowhere - and that’s the problem”.

He added: “The vote was overwhelming. My members in London are three to one in favour at least of him going, especially younger members who are fed up seeing someone who lacks the energy and capability to run the Labour Party.

”They have seen the lacklustre campaign for the EU and that affects their futures.

”They want a capable leader who can unite the party for the general election when it comes.”

Christina Rees, who was a Shadow Justice Minister, has resigned from Labour’s frontbench.

She’s number 54.

Sarah Champion has resigned

Labour Shadow Minister for Preventing Abuse, Sarah Champion, has resigned.

17:16

Alastair Campbell: "We are becoming a laughing stock"

Labour’s Alastair Campbell has had a passionate rant about Jeremy Corbyn and the future of the Labour party on BBC News

He said: “We are becoming a laughing stock. The Labour party is becoming a laughing stock. The country is becoming a laughing stock because of the lack of leadership that’s being shown in so many different places.

“And what it says to me is that he’s decided to put his own vanity ahead of the possibility of the party that he claims to love being utterly wiped out at an election that could be weeks, months away.”

He added: “He got the mandate. He was elected leader with a massive majority. Correct. Does he have the ability to lead? That is a bigger question, I’m afraid. And sometimes leadership is about being honest with yourself. Can I do this job?

“He cannot do the job.” The former New Labour spin doctor went on: “His supporters are saying it’s all orchestrated, it’s a coup, blah blah blah. These people who have been resigning over the last few days. Some of them I know, some of them I don’t know. They have seen close up - they have tried to make it work. They know it cannot work.

“And they’ve also seen that this new politics he’s talking about - I believe in a lot of the things Jeremy Corbyn says. I believe in trying to deal with division and inequality in society. I believe in trying to make the world a fairer place. Where’s the policy coming from? Where’s the leadership on policy coming from? Where is the arguments, other than doing what he did last night? Going to speak to the converted.

“I don’t know if you saw that documentary, that Vice film. Half an hour of going from hall to hall, telling the converted what they think. Did you ever see him going out to talk to small businesses about what they’re trying to do? “Do you ever see him trying to reach out to new support and trying to get it? We’re going to get wiped out if he stays on as leader.”

16:57

Corbyn's defiant statement in full

Jeremy Corbyn has released a statement in response to the vote of no confidence in his leadership, which he lost by 130 votes.

In the aftermath of last week’s referendum, our country faces major challenges. Risks to the economy and living standards are growing. The public is divided.

The government is in disarray. Ministers have made it clear they have no exit plan, but are determined to make working people pay with a new round of cuts and tax rises.

Labour has the responsibility to give a lead where the government will not. We need to bring people together, hold the government to account, oppose austerity and set out a path to exit that will protect jobs and incomes.

To do that we need to stand together. Since I was elected leader of our party nine months ago, we have repeatedly defeated the government over its attacks on living standards.

Last month, Labour become the largest party in the local elections. In Thursday’s referendum, a narrow majority voted to leave, but two thirds of Labour supporters backed our call for a remain vote.

I was democratically elected leader of our party for a new kind of politics by 60% of Labour members and supporters, and I will not betray them by resigning. Today’s vote by MPs has no constitutional legitimacy.

We are a democratic party, with a clear constitution. Our people need Labour party members, trade unionists and MPs to unite behind my leadership at a critical time for our country.

16:46

Jeremy Corbyn loses no confidence vote 172-40

Jeremy Corbyn has lost a vote of no confidence in his leadership of the Labour Party .

The result of the secret ballot was announced this afternoon, revealing 172 Labour MPs had voted against him, with just 40 MPs supporting him.

Four MPs abstained in the vote.

As incumbent leader, Mr Corbyn is guaranteed a place on the ballot in a leadership contest following the vote.

But only having the support of 40 MPs would not be enough to make it onto the ballot in a regular contest.

MPs standing in a Labour leadership election need the support of 20% of the Parliamentary Party and European Parliamentary Party - which is 50 MPs.
