Obama says no need for Brexit 'hysteria' EU has just pushed a pause button

Gregory Korte
Updated Jun 30, 2016 – 10.16am, first published at 9.53am
 or Subscribe to save article
EmailLinkedInTwitterFacebook

President Barack Obama has cautioned against "hysteria" over the UK vote to leave the European Union, saying all of Europe needs to take a breath and reassess how to preserve national identity while taking advantage of political and economic integration.

"I think that the best way to think about this is, a pause button has been pressed on the project of full European integration," Obama told National Public Radio in an interview broadcast on Tuesday. "I don't anticipate that there's going to be major cataclysmic changes as a result of this."
